Given a family of real functions F we say that a set M ⊂ ℝ is magic for F if for all f, g ∈ F we have f[M] ⊂ g[M] ⟹ f = g. This notion was introduced by Diamond, Pomerance and Rubel in 1981 [1] and investigated by many mathematicians, including S. Shelah, K. Ciesielski and M. Burke. Recently some results about magic sets were proved by Halbeisen, Lischka and Schumacher [2]. Inspired by their work I constructed two families of magic sets one of them being almost disjoint and the other one being independent. During my talk I will sketch the background, present the construction of one of those families, and show some general properties of magic sets.
