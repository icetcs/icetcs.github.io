In , Hjorth proved that for every countable ordinal α, there exists a complete ℒω1, ω-sentence ϕα that has models of all cardinalities less than or equal to ℵα, but no models of cardinality ℵα + 1. Unfortunately, his solution yields not one ℒω1, ω-sentence ϕα, but a set of ℒω1, ω-sentences, one of which is guaranteed to work.

The following is new: It is independent of the axioms of ${\rm{ZFC}}$ which of the Hjorth sentences works. More specifically, we isolate a diagonalization principle for functions from ω1 to ω1 which is a consequence of the Bounded Proper Forcing Axiom (${\rm{BPFA}}$) and then we use this principle to prove that Hjorth’s solution to characterizing ℵ2 in models of ${\rm{BPFA}}$ is different than in models of ${\rm{CH}}$.

This raises the question whether Hjorth’s result can be proved in an absolute way and what exactly this means, which we will discuss at the end of the talk.

This is joint work with Philipp Lücke.
